This guide walks you through signing up, creating a workspace, and deploying your first containerized app using App Launchpad. No Kubernetes knowledge required.

## Before you begin

You need a Sealos account. If you don't have one yet, go to [sealos.io](https://os.sealos.io) and sign up — it takes under a minute.

<Steps>

<Step title="Sign in to Sealos">

Navigate to [os.sealos.io](https://os.sealos.io). Sign in with GitHub, Google, WeChat, or a phone number — whichever you configured during sign-up.

After signing in, you land on the Sealos desktop. This is your cloud workspace: a browser-based interface with app icons for every Sealos service.

<Tip>
  The Sealos desktop works entirely in your browser. No local CLI or Kubernetes tooling is needed for this guide.
</Tip>
</Step>

<Step title="Create a workspace">

Sealos automatically provisions a personal workspace for you on first login. Your workspace is an isolated Kubernetes namespace with its own resource quotas and billing.

To create a team workspace or switch between workspaces:

1. Click your username or avatar in the top-right corner of the desktop.
2. Select **Switch workspace** from the dropdown.
3. Click **Create workspace**, enter a name, and confirm.

<Note>
  Each workspace has independent resource quotas, members, and billing. Resources in one workspace are invisible to other workspaces.
</Note>
</Step>

<Step title="Open App Launchpad">

App Launchpad is the easiest way to deploy any Docker image on Sealos. It creates a Kubernetes Deployment and exposes it through an HTTPS Ingress automatically.

1. On the Sealos desktop, click the **App Launchpad** icon.
2. Click **Create App** in the top-right corner.
</Step>

<Step title="Configure and deploy nginx">

Fill in the deployment form:

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **App name** | `my-nginx` |
| **Image** | `nginx:latest` |
| **CPU** | 0.1 cores |
| **Memory** | 64 MB |
| **Container port** | `80` |

Enable the **Public access** toggle. Sealos will generate a unique HTTPS URL for your app.

Click **Deploy** to launch the application.

```bash
# The image Sealos will pull and run:
nginx:latest
```

<Note>
  Sealos pulls public images from Docker Hub by default. For private registries, click **Advanced settings** and provide your registry credentials.
</Note>
</Step>

<Step title="Access your deployed app">

After clicking **Deploy**, Sealos takes you to the app details page. Wait for the status indicator to show **Running** — this typically takes 15–30 seconds.

Once running:

1. Find the **Public address** field in the app details panel.
2. Click the generated URL (for example, `https://my-nginx.cloud.sealos.run`).

You should see the nginx welcome page served over HTTPS. Sealos provisions a TLS certificate and configures the ingress automatically.

<Tip>
  You can update environment variables, scale replicas, or change resource limits from the app details page without redeploying.
</Tip>
</Step>

</Steps>

## Optional: Connect a database

If your app needs a database, Sealos provides managed PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB, and Redis clusters.

<Steps>

<Step title="Create a database">

1. Return to the Sealos desktop and click **Database**.
2. Click **Create database**, select a database type (for example, PostgreSQL), and configure your instance size.
3. Click **Deploy**. The database will be ready in about a minute.
</Step>

<Step title="Connect to your app">

From the database details page, copy the connection string. It follows this format:

```bash
postgresql://username:password@hostname:5432/dbname
```

In App Launchpad, open your app and click **Edit**. Add the connection string as an environment variable:

```bash
DATABASE_URL=postgresql://username:password@hostname:5432/dbname
```

Save and redeploy. Your app now has access to the managed database within the same workspace network.

<Note>
  Database instances in the same workspace communicate over the internal cluster network — no public exposure required for backend connections.
</Note>
</Step>

</Steps>
